About

As of 2018, over 11,000 restaurants were operating in the Greater Boston region, and faced with the challenges to survive, each restaurant does its best to stand out. From the uniqueness of the dishes to the ambience and location, everything is utilized by restaurants to ensure that customers are satisfied and come back for more. Customers also face challenges when trying to pick 1 out of 11,000 restaurants to dine at. Preferences such as cuisine, price, distance, and even time of day become important deciding factors for locals. But for tourists unfamiliar with Boston, navigating through Boston’s cityscape let alone picking a restaurant to dine at becomes a challenge. To help both tourists and locals, we utilize datasets relating to the city of Boston, and based on where the user wants to start their day in Boston, we find and recommend restaurants. To see which restaurant are safer to eat at we use a dataset from Analyze Boston containing food inspection violations that go back as far as 2007. To see how popular each restaurant is, we use data from Yelp’s businesses API and collect data on restaurant and bars around Boston. And finally, we use Google Maps’ Geocoding API to evaluate distances between recommended bars, and the user’s starting point. Using these datasets, we use constraints to choose restaurants to recommend.
